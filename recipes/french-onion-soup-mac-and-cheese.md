# French Onion Soup Mac and Cheese

> We&#39;ve combined French and American comfort foods to create this baked French onion soup mac and cheese. It&#39;s loaded with melty Gruyère and caramelized onions and has a panko–fried onion topping that gets wonderfully crispy and browned when baked.

## Ingredients

- 1 pound ( 456 g ) uncooked rotini pasta (about 5 1/2 cups )
- Kosher salt
- 11 tablespoons unsalted butter , divided ( 5 1/2 ounces ; 156 g)
- 1 1/2 pounds yellow or mixed onions ( 720 g ; about 3 medium ), thinly sliced lengthwise ( 6 cups )
- 1 1/2 pounds ( 720 g ) Gruyère cheese
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) dry sherry
- 1 1/2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme leaves, divided, plus more for garnish
- 1/3 cup all-purpose flour ( 1 1/2 ounces ; 40 g )
- 6 cups ( 1.35 L ) whole milk
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon ( 18 g ) roasted beef bouillon (such as Better Than Bouillon)
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1 cup panko bread crumbs ( 2 1/2 ounces ; 72 g )
- 1/2 cup store-bought crispy fried onions ( 1 ounce ; 30 g ), coarsely crushed


## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 400°F (205°) In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook pasta until just shy of al dente, about 2 minutes less than cooking time indicated on package. Drain, then transfer pasta to a large mixing bowl; set aside.

2. While pasta is cooking, in a Dutch oven, heat 4 tablespoons (60 g) butter over medium heat until melted. Add onions and cook, covered, stirring occasionally, until onions are soft and translucent, 6 to 8 minutes. Uncover and reduce heat to medium-low. Continue cooking, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until onions become very soft and brown, 30 to 35 minutes. If browned onion juices on bottom of pot begin to burn, add 1 tablespoon (15ml) water, scrape up browned bits, and continue cooking; add water whenever needed to prevent burning.

3. While onions caramelize, shred 1 pound (456 g) of Gruyère and cube remaining 8 ounces (226 g) into 1/2-inch pieces. Set aside until ready to use.

4. Once onions have browned, increase heat to medium and stir in sherry 1 tablespoon thyme; cook, stirring constantly, until fragrant and liquid has evaporated, 1 to 2 minutes. Remove from heat and transfer to a bowl.

5. Return now-empty Dutch oven to medium heat and melt 4 tablespoons (60 g) butter; add flour and cook, stirring constantly, until raw flour scent is gone, about 1 minute. Add milk in a thin, steady stream, whisking constantly and thoroughly and getting into all corners of the pan to create a smooth, lump-free texture. Sauce will initially become very thick, then get very thin once all the milk is added. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, whisking often, and cook until sauce coats the back of a spoon, about 15 minutes. Whisk in mustard, bouillon, and pepper.

6. Remove from heat and, working in increments, whisk in shredded Gruyère until a smooth, emulsified cheese sauce forms; stir in reserved onions.

7. Scrape cheese sauce into pasta and mix until evenly coated. Let cool slightly, about 5 minutes, then add cubed Gruyère and mix well. Scrape pasta into a greased 9- by 13-inch baking dish and smooth surface into an even layer.

8. In a small bowl, combine panko, fried onions, and remaining 1/2 tablespoon thyme. Melt remaining 3 tablespoons (45 g) butter, then add to panko mixture and mix until evenly coated. Scatter panko mixture evenly over surface of mac and cheese. Bake until browned and bubbling, 12 to 15 minutes.

9. Let mac and cheese rest for 15 minutes. Garnish with thyme leaves and serve.

10. Large pasta pot, Dutch oven, 9- by 13-inch baking dish

11. The pasta can be cooked, cooled, and refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days before preparing the casserole.

12. Cheese can be shredded and cubed up to 1 week ahead and refrigerated in separate airtight containers.

13. The onions can be sliced ahead up to 1 day ahead and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator or fully caramelized up to 1 week ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container.
