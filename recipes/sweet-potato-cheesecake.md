## Instructions

1. This recipe requires an 8- by 4-inch nonreactive aluminum pan, preferably of a non-latching, two-piece style, such as this one . Otherwise, a traditional cake pan of those dimensions will work, with a touch more effort to unmold. Of course, cheesecake can be baked in any size or style of pan, but the volume of batter, target temperatures, and times will require individual adjustments, which have not been tested here.

2. Food processor (see notes).

3. This recipe works best with moist sweet potato varieties such as Garnet, Jewel, or Beauregard. If you use dry sweet potato varieties, the texture will be more grainy and cake-like. While you can use our preferred frozen-roasting method linked to in the ingredient list to cook the sweet potatoes, you can also simply roast them following these instructions: Adjust oven rack to center position. Place 1 large sheet of heavy-duty aluminum foil on a work surface. Place potatoes in center, fold up foil and crimp edges to seal tightly. Transfer pouches to a rimmed baking sheet and place in oven. Set oven to 300°F (150°C). Roast until a thin skewer inserted into potatoes meets no resistance, about 2 hours. Remove potatoes from oven and set aside until cool enough to handle.

4. In an airtight container, the cheesecake batter can be refrigerated for up to 2 days and used straight from the fridge.
