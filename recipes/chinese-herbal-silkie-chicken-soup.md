# Chinese Herbal Silkie Chicken Soup

> Silkie chicken has striking dark skin and a delicious concentrated chicken flavor. Simmered in this soup with traditional Chinese medicinal (T.C.M.) ingredients and other aromatics, it produces one of the most deeply flavorful chicken soups.

## Ingredients

- 1 whole silkie chicken (about 2 1/2 pounds ; 1.1 kg ), gutted (see notes)
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) Shaoxing wine
- 1 tablespoon ( 12 g ) kosher salt , plus more as needed
- 1 1/2 ounces ( 40 g ; about 12 ( 1-inch )) dried red jujubes (da zao)
- 1 ounce ( 25 g ) dried shiitake mushrooms , preferably sliced (about 2/3 cup or 9 shiitakes if whole)
- 1 ounce ( 25 g ; about 1/4 cup ) dried goji berries
- 3/4 ounce ( 20 g ; about 4 ( 3 1/2-inch) pieces ) dried Chinese yam (huai shan; optional)
- 1/3 ounce ( 9 g ; about 6 slices ) dried sliced angelica root (dong quai)
- 1/3 ounce ( 10 g ; about 1/8 cup ) dried lily bulb (bai he; see notes)
- 4 medium scallions (about 2 ounces ; 60 g ), trimmed and chopped
- 1 1/2 ounces ( 45 g ; about 1 ( 5-inch) knob ) peeled fresh ginger , thinly sliced
- 1/3 ounce ( 10 g ) dried scallops (about 8 fingernail-size scallops ), roughly chopped
- 1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper
- White soy sauce , to taste (optional; see notes)
- 3/4 ounce ( 20 g ; about 6 ( 1-inch )) dried red jujubes (da zao), pitted and sliced
- 1/2 ounce ( 15 g ; about 3 tablespoons ) dried goji berries
- Sliced scallion s, as desired


## Instructions

1. In a large stockpot or Dutch oven, cover silkie chicken with cold water, set over high heat, and bring to a boil. Remove from heat. Drain, then transfer chicken to a large bowl of cold water until cool. Drain well. Rub chicken all over with Shaoxing wine and 1 tablespoons salt.

2. Meanwhile, in a medium heatproof bowl, combine jujubes, shiitakes, goji berries, Chinese yam (if using), angelica root, and lily bulb. Pour 1 2/3 cup (400ml) boiling water all over and let stand until rehydrated, about 15 minutes. If using whole shiitakes, cut into slices after rehydrating.

3. Clean stockpot or Dutch oven and return silkie chicken to it along with any Shaoxing wine. Add rehydrated aromatics and their soaking liquid along with scallions, ginger, dried scallops, and white pepper. Cover with 4 quarts (4L) cold water and set over medium heat until gently simmering; skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Reduce heat to maintain gentle simmer and cook until chicken is cooked through and meat can be pulled from bones easily, about 45 minutes.

4. Carefully remove chicken from pot and transfer to a work surface until cool enough to handle, about 5 minutes. Using your hands, shred meat and skin from chicken and place in a small bowl; refrigerate until ready to use. Return carcass to broth, cover, and cook at a very gentle simmer for a clearer broth, about 3 hours, or a light boil for a creamier broth, about 2 hours. Strain broth, discarding chicken carcass and aromatics.

5. Return stock to cleaned pot and season with salt and/or white soy sauce.

6. For Garnish: In a small heatproof bowl, combine jujubes and goji berries and add enough boiling water to just cover. Let stand until rehydrated, about 2 minutes.

7. When ready to serve, add shredded chicken meat to broth and reheat until simmering. Divide jujube, goji berry, and scallion garnishes among serving bowls and ladle broth and chicken meat on top. Serve.

8. Silkie chickens are often sold cleaned, but with their collagen-rich feet and head still attached; leave those parts on for a silkier soup.

9. Take care not to confuse dried lily bulb (which is called for in this recipe) with dried lily bud.

10. White soy sauce is a Japanese, light-colored soy sauce. While not a traditional Chinese ingredient, its light color and flavor work well with the soup.

11. The broth and chicken can be refrigerated together for up to 5 days. Reheat gently and prepare garnish shortly before serving.
