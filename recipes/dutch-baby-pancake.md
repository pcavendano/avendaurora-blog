# Dutch Baby Pancake

> This Dutch baby has a custardy center and crispy edges, and it comes together with minimal effort—perfect for an easy yet impressive brunch.

## Ingredients

- 4 large eggs , at room temperature
- 3/4 cup ( 180 ml ) whole milk , at room temperature
- 3 1/3 ounces all-purpose flour ( 96 g ; 3/4 cup )
- 1 ounce granulated sugar ( 30 g ; 2 tablespoons )
- 1/2 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1 ounce unsalted butter ( 2 tablespoons ; 28 g )
- 2 1/2 ounces cold crème fraîche ( 70 g ; 5 tablespoons )
- 1/2 ounce conf ectioner &#39; sugar ( 16 g ; 2 tablespoons )
- 1 teaspoon vanilla paste (see notes)
- 1/8 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- Confectioners&#39; sugar
- Whipped Crème Fraîche , see above
- Lemon wedges , for serving
- Fresh blueberries and raspberries , optional


## Instructions

1. For the Batter: In a blender, blend eggs, milk, flour, sugar, and salt until smooth, about 20 seconds. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 8 hours.

2. When oven has preheated, remove skillet from oven, and add butter; using gloved hands or oven mitts, carefully swirl skillet to coat bottom and edges of pan with butter until completely melted, about 20 seconds. Pour batter into pan and bake for 10 minutes. With skillet still in oven, reduce oven temperature to 400°F (205°C) and bake until golden brown and significantly puffed around edges, 8 to 10 minutes more.

3. To Serve: Remove Dutch baby from oven. Using a small fine-mesh sieve, dust pancake with confectioners&#39; sugar, and serve with whipped crème fraîche, lemon wedges, and fresh berries, if desired. Serve immediately.
