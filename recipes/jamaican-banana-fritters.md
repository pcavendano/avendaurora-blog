# Jamaican Banana Fritters

> Use overripe fruit to make Jamaican banana fritters that are tender yet slightly chewy—and even simpler than basic pancakes.

## Ingredients

- 1 1/2 cups mashed overripe bananas ( 14 ounces ; 395 g ) from 4 medium bananas
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ( 7 ml ) vanilla extract
- 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour ( 6 3/4 ounces ; 192 g )
- 3/4 cup ( 175 ml ) milk or water, plus more as needed
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) vegetable oil , plus more as needed
- 1 1/2 tablespoons ( 20 g ) granulated sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- Kosher salt , to taste


## Instructions

1. In a medium bowl, stir bananas, vanilla extract, and nutmeg to combine. Whisk in flour, followed by milk or water, until a thick and slightly lumpy batter begins to form. (Exactly how thick varies from cook to cook, but a general goal is a batter that’s thicker than pancake batter, spreadable, but not free-flowing.) If needed, add additional liquid, 1 tablespoon at a time, to achieve the desired texture.

2. In a large sauté or frying pan or nonstick skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Working in batches to avoid crowding the pan, carefully add a 1-ounce spoonfuls (about 2 tablespoons) of banana batter to oil, making sure to release them close to oil to prevent splashing. Fry until golden on first side, 45 seconds to 1 minute. Flip and fry until golden on second side side, 45 seconds to 1 minute longer. Transfer to paper towels to drain, and repeat with remaining batter, adding oil as needed if pan is dry and adjusting heat to prevent over browning.

3. In a small bowl, stir sugar and cinnamon to combine. Transfer fritters to a serving dish and sprinkle cinnamon-sugar and salt to taste all over hot fritters. Serve hot or at room temperature as a side dish with a meal or as a quick and tasty snack.

5. The banana fritters are best eaten immediately.
