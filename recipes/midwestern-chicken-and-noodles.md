# Midwestern Chicken and Noodles

> Don’t confuse Midwestern chicken and noodles with soup. This dish features juicy hunks of chicken in a roux-thickened gravy with chewy, homemade egg noodles, all served over fluffy mashed potatoes.

## Ingredients

- 9 ounces all-purpose flour ( 255 g ; 2 cups ), plus more for dusting
- 8 yolks from 8 large eggs ( 6.5 ounces ; 180 g ;)
- 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1 teaspoon or up to 2 tablespoons water
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) extra-virgin olive oil
- 3 bone-in split chicken breasts , skin removed (about 1 pound ; 454 g each)
- 4 tablespoons ( 60 g ) butter
- 4 tablespoons ( 32 g ) all-purpose flour
- 4 cups ( 945 ml ) homemade chicken stock or store-bought low-sodium chicken broth
- 1 cup ( 240 ml ) water
- 2 teaspoons freshly ground black pepper
- 2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1/2 cup ( 120 ml ) heavy whipping cream
- 1 double recipe of Kenji’s ultra fluffy mashed potatoes or at least 6 cups (about 1.5 kg ) of warmed mashed potatoes of your choice


## Instructions

1. After the rest, divide dough into 2 equal pieces. Set 1 piece of dough on a lightly floured work surface and keep the other piece covered to prevent drying. Using a rolling pin, roll dough out to a 12-inch round. Cut round in half to form two semicircles, then cut each semicircle crosswise into 1/4 -wide strips. Toss cut noodles with a small amount of flour to prevent sticking and set aside until ready to use.

2. For the Chicken and Gravy : In a large Dutch oven, heat oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Season chicken generously with salt and pepper on both sides. Add chicken, breast-side down, and cook without moving until chicken is deeply browned and releases easily from pan, about 7 minutes. Flip chicken and cook until the underside of breasts is also deeply browned, adjusting heat as needed to avoid scorching, about 5 minutes. Transfer chicken to plate and set aside.

3. Add butter to now-empty Dutch oven and heat until melted and foaming subsides, about 30 seconds. Add flour and stir until fully incorporated and raw flour smell has cooked off, about 30 seconds longer. While whisking constantly to avoid lumps, slowly add chicken broth and water in a thin stream. Bring gravy to a boil, then return chicken and any juices to pot and lower heat to a gentle simmer. Cook, flipping chicken halfway through cooking, until the thickest part of the chicken breast reads 150°F (65°C) with an instant-read thermometer, 20 to 25 minutes. Remove pot from heat. Using tongs, transfer chicken from gravy to a large heat-proof bowl.

4. Using 2 forks, shred chicken and discard bones. Return pot to medium-high heat and add noodles. Simmer, stirring occasionally to prevent clumping, until noodles are tender, 10 to 15 minutes.

5. Return chicken to pot, stir to combine, and remove from heat. Thin with up to 1/2 cup (120 ml) additional broth, if needed, to achieve a thick, velvety gravy that coats the chicken and noodles. Stir in cream and season to taste with salt and pepper. To serve, spoon a generous portion of chicken, noodles, and gravy over plates of mashed potatoes.
