# Chess Pie

> This is a great, basic recipe for Chess Pie, the gooey, sweet Southern classic. The sweet, rich custard filling is balanced with a touch of white vinegar. A small amount of flour and cornmeal create its signature thin, crispy caramelized crust.

## Ingredients

- 1 half recipe Easy Pie Dough , shaped in a 9-inch pie plate and chilled for at least 2 hours
- 4 large eggs ( 7 ounces ; 200 g ), room temperature
- 1/2 cup ( 112 g ) unsalted butter , melted and cooled
- 1/3 cup ( 79 ml ) whole milk
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) vanilla extract
- 2 cups (14 ounces ; 400 g ) granulated sugar
- 2 tablespoons finely ground cornmeal ( 2/3 ounce ; 18 g )
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour ( 1/3 ounce ; 8 g )
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) white vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt ; for table salt use half as much by volume


## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to the lowest position and preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Lightly prick chilled pie shell all over bottom in 1/2-inch intervals. Line pie shell with a double layer 14-inch square of parchment paper or aluminum foil. Fill with sugar, pie weights, or dried beans.

2. Transfer to a parchment or foil-lined rimmed baking sheet and bake for 10 minutes. Reduce oven temperature to 325°F (160°C). Carefully remove liner and pie weights from crust and continue to bake until the crust is cooked through and just starting to turn golden brown, 5 to 8 minutes. Set aside and let cool completely, about 30 minutes.

3. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, butter, milk, and vanilla together until smooth. Pour sugar into the egg mixture in a slow steady stream, whisking as you go until well combined. Whisk cornmeal, flour, vinegar, and salt into the mixture until smooth.

4. Pour the filling into the parbaked pie shell. Bake until the filling is lightly browned and center of the pie is just set, but still jiggles slightly when shaken, and register 180℉(82℃), 45 to 50 minutes. If necessary, shield edges of crust with aluminum foil to prevent overbrowning. Transfer to a wire rack and let cool completely, about 4 hours. Slice and serve.

5. 9-inch pie plate

6. Pie can be refrigerated for up to 4 days. The pie can be served chilled or at room temperature.
