# Beef Tongue Tacos With Consomé (Tacos de Lengua y Consomé)

> These tacos de lengua pair crisped beef tongue with a rich, aromatic consomé. Slow-cooked, deeply beefy, and easier than you think to make at home.

## Ingredients

- 1 whole beef tongue (3 to 4 pounds; 1.4–1.8 kg))
- 1 large white onion ( 10 ounces ; 283 g ), peeled and halved
- 2 medium carrots ( 8 ounces ; 225 g ), peeled and halved crosswise
- 2 large celery sticks ( 3 1/2 ounces ; 100 g ), halved crosswise
- 1 bunch of cilantro stems ( 1 1/2 ounces ; 40 g ), thoroughly washed
- 6 medium garlic cloves
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon whole black peppercorns
- 1 tablespoon whole coriander seeds
- 1 teaspoon whole cumin seeds
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Warm tortillas
- Cilantro leaves
- Finely diced white onion
- Lime wedges
- Salsa verde , optional


## Instructions

1. In a large pot, cover beef tongue with 4 quarts (4 L) water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes, occasionally skimming scum that rises to the surface.

2. Add halved onion, carrots, celery, cilantro stems, garlic cloves, bay leaves, black peppercorns, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, and 1 tablespoon (9 g) salt. Cover and cook at a gentle simmer until the tongue can be easily pierced with a fork, 4 to 5 hours.

3. Large pot, fine-mesh strainer
