# Pineapple Upside Down Cake

> This classic retro pineapple upside down cake is soft and buttery with a glossy and caramelized brown sugar, pineapple, and cherry topping.

## Ingredients

- One 20 -ounce (567g) can of pineapple sliced rings packed in 100% pineapple juice, plus 3/4 cup (180ml) juice reserved
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) unrefined virgin coconut oil
- 8 tablespoons ( 113 g) unsalted butter, divided
- 4 2/3 ounces (133g) granulated sugar (about 2/3 cup)
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 ml) spiced rum, such as Captain Morgan brand (optional, see notes)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla bean paste or vanilla extract
- 2 large eggs , at room temperatur e
- 5 ounces (141g) dark brown sugar (about 2/3 cup packed)
- 7 maraschino cherries , stems removed and patted dry
- 6 3/4 ounces (191g) all-purpose flour (about 1 1/2 cups)
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt; for table salt use half as much by volume
- 1/2 cup ( 12 0ml) buttermilk


## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 350°F(175℃). In a small saucepan, bring 3/4 cup reserved canned pineapple juice to a simmer over medium-high heat. Once simmering, reduce heat to medium, and continue to simmer until reduced to about 1/4 cup, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat, and whisk in coconut oil and 4 tablespoons butter until melted. Transfer to a large heatproof bowl. Whisk in granulated sugar, rum, and vanilla into the melted butter mixture until smooth; whisk in eggs until incorporated. Set aside while preparing the skillet.

2. In a 10-inch cast-iron skillet, melt the remaining 4 tablespoons butter over medium heat. Once melted, add brown sugar, and cook, stirring constantly, until sugar is melted and starts to bubble, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat. Arrange 7 of the pineapple rings over the sugar mixture in the skillet. Cut 1 of the remaining pineapple rings into 1-inch pieces; place around the rings to fill in any gaps. (Reserve any remaining pineapple slices for another use.) Place one cherry in the center of each pineapple ring.

3. In a medium bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt until combined. Add half of the flour mixture to the egg mixture and use a spatula to fold until combined. Stir in the buttermilk until combined. Add the remaining flour mixture, then fold until the cake batter is combined.

4. Pour the batter over the prepared pineapple rings in the skillet, and spread into a smooth even layer. Bake until the cake is golden brown and a wooden skewer inserted into the center comes out clean, 30 to 40 minutes. (If the top becomes too brown before the cake is baked through, tent with aluminum foil to finish baking.)

5. Transfer skillet to a wire rack and let cool slightly for 15 minutes. Run a small palette knife around the edges of the skillet to loosen, then place a serving plate over the cake and carefully invert the cake onto the plate. (If any fruit sticks to the skillet, remove and position on top of the cake.) Let the cake cool for at least 30 minutes. Slice and serve warm or at room temperature with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream, if desired.

7. The rum can be replaced with 1 tablespoon water.

8. Cover and store at room temperature for up to three days.
