# Jellied Cranberry Sauce

> Liven your holiday table up with this perfectly set, deeply flavorful jellied cranberry sauce. It&#39;s just as smooth and sliceable as canned cranberry sauce, but the flavor is so much better.

## Ingredients

- 3 cups fresh or frozen cranberries (12 ounces; 340 g)
- 1 1/4 cups ( 300 ml) water
- 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar (8 3/4 ounces; 250g)
- 1 teaspoon grated orange zest and/or grated fresh ginger (optional)


## Instructions

1. Place two small plates in the freezer to chill. In a large saucepan, combine cranberries and water. Bring to boil over medium-high heat.

2. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring occasionally with a rubber spatula, until cranberries soften and skins burst, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.

3. Set a food mill over a large heatproof bowl. Process cranberry mixture through food mill, discarding skins. (If you don’t have a food mill, transfer mixture to a blender and blend until smooth, then strain through a fine-mesh strainer into a bowl.)

4. Return cranberry purée to now-empty saucepan. Stir in sugar and orange zest and/or ginger (if using) and stir to combine. Bring to a boil over medium heat. Cook, stirring and scraping bottom of pot often, until mixture thickens and reaches 217 to 221°F (103 to 105°C) on an instant-read thermometer, 10 to 15 minutes (mixture may spatter as it cooks down; reduce heat to medium-low if needed). Skim and remove any foam that rises to the top while cooking. Remove from heat.

5. To test if jelly is set, spoon about 1 teaspoon sauce onto chilled plate. Return to freezer for 1 minute to cool to room temperature. Run your finger through mixture on plate; if clear trail is left behind without closing up and mixture wrinkles when pushed, then the sauce is set. If not, return mixture to boil and continue to cook until thickened, 2 to 5 minutes, then repeat plate test with remaining chilled plate.

6. Pour cranberry sauce into 2-cup mold, small 2-cup capacity bowl, or a clean, empty 15-ounce can. Let cool at room temperature until just warm, about 30 minutes.

7. Cover sauce with plastic wrap and transfer to refrigerator. Chill until firm, at least 4 hours.

8. When ready to serve, remove plastic wrap, then run a small offset spatula or butter knife down sides of mold to gently loosen sauce from edges of mold and break suction. Place a flat plate or platter on top of mold. Invert quickly to release sauce, rapping plate or platter sharply on counter if needed to release. (If cranberry sauce still sticks in mold, re-invert, gently loosen sides, and try again.) Slice if desired and serve.

9. Large saucepan, rubber spatula, food mill (or blender and fine-mesh strainer), instant-read thermometer, 2-cup jelly mold (or small bowl or clean, empty 15-ounce can)

10. If you’re using frozen cranberries, there’s no need to thaw them before cooking.

11. Warm spices like cinnamon, allspice, cloves, or cardamom also pair well with cranberry sauce. If you’d like to add a spice to this dish, either in combination with the orange or ginger or alone, start by adding 1/4 teaspoon of a ground spice at the beginning of step 4, taste at the end of that step, and add more as desired. Spice blends like pumpkin pie spice, apple pie spice, five-spice powder, or garam masala would all be fun here as well.

12. The sauce can be made through the end of step 7 and refrigerated for up to 4 days before serving.
