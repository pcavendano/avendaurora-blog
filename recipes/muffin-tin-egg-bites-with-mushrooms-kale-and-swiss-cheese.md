# Muffin Tin Egg Bites With Mushrooms, Kale, and Swiss Cheese

> These silky muffin tin egg bites are loaded with mushrooms, kale, and Swiss cheese. They’re great for Sunday brunch or for a make-ahead weekday breakfast.

## Ingredients

- Cooking spray
- 7 large eggs
- 2/3 cup 2% small-curd cottage cheese ( 6 ounces ; 170 g )
- 3 ounces ( 90 g ) Swiss cheese , shredded (about 3/4 cup )
- 1 1/2 teaspoons rice starch or cornstarch
- 1 teaspoon hot sauce , optional
- 1 teaspoon ( 3 g ) Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt use half as much by volume
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- 3 tablespoon ( 45 ml ) canola oil , divided
- 1 2/3 cups finely chopped portobello mushroom caps , from 2 medium (3 1/2-inch) caps ( 5 ounces ; 140 g )
- 2 cups lightly packed stemmed and chopped lacinatio kale ( 2 ounces ; 60 g )
- Boiling water for water bath


## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to middle positions, and preheat oven to 325°F (160℃). Lightly coat a 12-cup muffin pan with cooking spray; set aside.

2. In a blender jar, combine eggs, cottage cheese, Swiss cheese, rice starch or cornstarch, hot sauce (if using), salt, black pepper, and garlic powder and blend on medium speed until smooth, about 30 seconds. Pour 2 cups of the egg mixture into a liquid measuring cup; set aside. Leave remaining egg mixture in the blender jar.

3. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add mushrooms, and cook, stirring often, until lightly browned, about 6 minutes; transfer to a large bowl. Add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to skillet. Reduce heat to medium, and add kale. Cook, stirring often, until wilted, about 2 minutes. Scrape kale into blender jar with remaining egg mixture. Process on medium speed until kale is very finely chopped, about 10 seconds. Stir kale and egg mixture into mushrooms in bowl.

4. Divide vegetable-egg mixture into prepared muffin cups. Gently pour reserved egg mixture over mushroom-kale-egg mixture.

5. Set filled muffin tin in a roasting pan. Place roasting pan on middle oven rack and pour boiling water around muffin tin until water is 1 inch deep, about halfway up the sides of the muffin tin. Bake until the center of the egg bites look dry, with some bubbles on the surface, but edges are still wet, 10 minutes. Carefully remove roasting pan with muffin tin from oven, and using a toothpick or small spoon, gently stir each egg bite, combining the soft egg curds with the wet egg mixture. Return to oven, and continue to bake until eggs are completely set, about 20 minutes longer. Carefully remove muffin pan from roasting pan. Let cool for 5 minutes. Run a small offset spatula or butter knife around the edges of each egg bite to loosen, then gently turn the pan upside down to remove the egg bites. Serve or let cool completely before storing.
