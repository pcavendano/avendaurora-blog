# Sheet-Pan Fried Rice

> This easy sheet-pan fried rice recipe with alliums, carrots, and peas delivers crispy, golden grains without a wok. It’s fast, scalable, and perfect for a low-effort weeknight dinner.

## Recipe Details

## Ingredients

- 1 large onion , finely chopped ( 8 ounces ; 230 g )
- 2 medium carrots , peeled and cut into small dice ( 6 ounces ; 170 g )
- 4 scallions , ends trimmed and thinly sliced ( 2 ounces ; 60 g )
- 4 medium cloves garlic , minced ( 1 tablespoon ; 10 g )
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 ml ) vegetable or canola oil , divided
- Kosher salt and ground white pepper
- 4 cups cooked white rice ( 24 ounces ; 700 g ; see notes), grains broken up and separated by hand
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 ml ) low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 teaspoons ( 10 ml ) fish sauce
- 2 teaspoons ( 10 ml ) toasted sesame oil
- 8 ounces ( 130 g ) frozen peas
- Fried eggs , for serving (optional)


## Instructions

1. Place rimmed baking sheet in oven and preheat oven to 450°F (230°C).

2. Meanwhile, in a medium bowl, stir together onion, carrot, scallions, and garlic with 1 tablespoon (15 ml) canola oil until thoroughly coated. Season with salt and white pepper.

3. Remove sheet pan from oven and quickly spread the vegetables on it in an even, single layer. Return to the oven and cook for 5 minutes. Toss vegetables, then return to oven for another 5 minutes.

4. Add rice to a medium bowl, drizzle with soy sauce, fish sauce, and sesame oil, and toss to coat.

5. Add rice to the sheet pan, mix with the vegetables, then spread in an even, single layer. Roast, stirring once halfway through, until the rice begins to toast on the outer edges, about 15 minutes.

6. Add the frozen peas, stir to distribute evenly, and return to oven until peas are heated through and the rice is crispy and chewy, but not burnt, about 5 minutes longer. Remove from the oven, top rice with fried egg, if using, and serve immediately.

7. For the best results, use Chinese-style medium-grain rice, jasmine rice, or sushi rice. Rice should either be cooked fresh, spread on a tray, and allowed to steam-dry for 20 minutes, or, alternatively, transferred to a loosely covered container and refrigerated for at least 12 hours and up to 3 days. Break large rice clumps up with your hands before beginning.

8. The fried rice is best made right before serving.
