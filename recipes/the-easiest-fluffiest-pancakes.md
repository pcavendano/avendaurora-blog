# The Easiest, Fluffiest Pancakes

> No frills, no tricks, no complicated steps—just solid technique for easy, thick pancakes that are fluffy, with a tender, pillowy soft crumb. They’re the ultimate weekend-worthy pancakes.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups all-purpose flour ( 9 ounces ; 255 g )
- 1/4 cup sugar ( 1.75 ounces ; 50 g )
- 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 3/4 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt use half as much by volume
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) neutral oil such as vegetable or canola oil, or melted butter, plus extra for greasing the skillet
- 1 1/3 cups ( 315 ml) whole milk , room temperature
- 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract , optional
- Assorted toppings , such as butter , maple syrup, confectioners&#39; sugar, honey, jams, preserves, sweetened whipped cream, or chocolate syrup


## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt together until combined. In a medium bowl, whisk eggs, oil, milk and vanilla (if using) until well combined. Pour egg mixture into flour mixture and with a whisk, stir gently until just combined (batter should remain lumpy with a few streaks of flour). Let batter sit for 10 to 15 minutes before cooking.

2. In a 12-inch nonstick skillet, heat 1 teaspoon oil over medium-low heat until shimmering, 5 minutes. Using paper towels, carefully wipe out excess oil, leaving thin film on bottom and sides of skillet.

3. To Test Temperature of Skillet: Drop 1 tablespoon batter in center of skillet. If pancake is pale golden brown after 1 minute, skillet is ready. If it is too light or too dark, adjust heat accordingly.

4. Using 1/4-cup dry measuring cup or ice cream scoop, portion batter into skillet in 3 places, leaving even spacing between portions. Use the back of a measuring cup or a spoon to gently spread batter into a 4-inch round. Cook until bubbles on surface are just beginning to break, edges are set, and bottom sides are golden brown, 2 to 4 minutes.

5. Using a thin, wide spatula, flip pancakes and continue to cook until the second sides are golden brown, 1 to 3 minutes longer. Transfer to a plate and serve. (Alternatively transfer cooked pancakes to a wire rack set in a rimmed baking sheet, and hold warm in a 200℉; 93℃ oven until ready to serve.) Repeat with remaining batter, using additional oil as needed.

6. 12-inch nonstick skillet or a well-seasoned cast iron or carbon steel skillet

7. To store leftover pancakes, separate them with paper or foil between each pancake before wrapping tightly in plastic wrap or storing in a zipper-lock freezer bag and freeze up to 3 weeks.
