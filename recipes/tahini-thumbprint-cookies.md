# Tahini Thumbprint Cookies

> Tahini and raspberry jam give these thumbprint cookies a nutty, toasty flavor and a bright, jammy center, all in a straightforward recipe ideal for holiday baking.

## Ingredients

- 255 g all-purpose flour ( 9 ounces ; 2 cups )
- 1/2 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1 teaspoons ( 4 g ) baking powder
- 8 tablespoons unsalted butter ( 4 ounces ; 113 g ), at room temperature
- 106 g light brown sugar ( 3 3/4 ounces ; 1/2 cup )
- 66 g granulated sugar ( 2 1/3 ounces ; 1/3 cup )
- 2 large egg whites , at room temperature, divided
- 1 large egg , at room temperature
- 1 teaspoon ( 5 ml ) vanilla extract
- 120 g tahini ( 4 1/4 ounces ; 1/2 cup ), at room temperature and stirred well (see notes)
- 6 tablespoons lightly toasted white sesame seeds ( 1 3/4 ounces ; 50 g )
- 120 g seedless raspberry jam ( 4 1/4 ounces ; 6 tablespoons )


## Instructions

1. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper and set aside. In a medium bowl, whisk flour, salt, and baking powder until combined.

2. In a large bowl with a hand mixer, or in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar on medium-high speed until light and fluffy, 2 to 3 minutes.

3. Add 1 egg white, whole egg, and vanilla, and beat on medium speed until combined. Add tahini and beat until smooth and creamy, about 30 seconds.

4. Add flour mixture in 2 additions, beating on low speed just until no dry streaks remain.

5. Place sesame seeds in a small bowl. Using a small ice cream scoop or a 1-tablespoon measuring spoon, portion cookie dough into 24 equal portions (about 31 g; 2 tablespoons each). Roll each portion between hands into a small ball. On a clean work surface, lightly press each ball with the bottom of a 1-cup measuring cup or coffee mug to flatten it to about 1/2 inch thick and roughly 2 inches in diameter. Working with one or two cookies at a time, lightly brush tops and sides of cookies with remaining egg white and place, brushed-side-down, in bowl with sesame seeds, pressing into seeds lightly to coat. Arrange 12 cookies on each prepared baking sheet, sesame-side up, spacing cookies 2 inches apart. Repeat with remaining cookies. Freeze cookies on sheet trays until firm, about 20 minutes.

6. Meanwhile, adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Remove 1 baking sheet from freezer and bake until edges are slightly set, about 6 minutes. (Leave the other cookie sheet in the freezer while the first one bakes.) Remove from oven and carefully press a shallow indentation in the middle of each cookie using the back of a 1/2-teaspoon measuring spoon or the rounded end of a wooden spoon handle. Fill each indentation with about 1/2 teaspoon raspberry jam.

7. Return to oven, and bake until edges are lightly golden and centers are set, 9 to 10 minutes.

8. Cool cookies on baking sheet for 5 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely, about 15 minutes. Repeat with remaining sheet of cookies. Serve.

9. 2 rimmed baking sheets, stand mixer or hand mixer, pastry brush, 2 wire racks

10. Tahini separates naturally as it sits, with the oil rising to the top. Be sure to stir it very well before measuring so the fat is evenly incorporated; using separated tahini can make the dough oily and cause the cookies to overspread. Tahini also varies from brand to brand—some are thinner and more pourable, while others are thicker and more paste-like. Stirring thoroughly ensures you get consistent results no matter which brand you use.
