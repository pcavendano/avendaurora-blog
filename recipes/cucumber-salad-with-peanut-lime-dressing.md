# Cucumber Salad with Peanut-Lime Dressing

> Make this bold, pad Thai–inspired cucumber salad with peanut-lime dressing, crisp veggies, and tons of flavor—plus get a simple trick to keep it from getting soggy.

## Ingredients

- 2 large English cucumbers ( 1 1/2 pounds ; 680 g total), sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds (see notes)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt , divided; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 3 tablespoons creamy, unsweetened peanut butter (see notes)
- 3 tablespoons ( 45 ml ) fresh lime juice (from 2 small limes ), plus lime wedges for serving
- 1 packed tablespoon light brown sugar , (about 1/2 ounce ; 14 g )
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) unseasoned rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) fish sauce
- 1 large garlic clove , minced
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) untoasted sesame oil
- 2 small carrots , peeled, ( 4 ounces ; 57 g total) and shredded on the largest holes of a box grater (about 1 cup packed )
- 2 medium scallions , trimmed and thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro ( 1/2 ounce ; 14 g )
- 1 small ( 18 g ) Fresno or jalapeño chile , seeded and finely chopped
- 1/2 cup salted, roasted peanuts ( 2 1/2 ounces ; 70 g ), chopped, divided


## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, add cucumbers and 1 teaspoon kosher salt; toss to combine. Transfer cucumbers to a colander and set over now-empty bowl. Set aside for 10 minutes.

2. Meanwhile, in a medium bowl, whisk peanut butter, lime juice, brown sugar, vinegar, fish sauce, garlic, and remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt until smooth. Slowly whisk in sesame oil until emulsified; set aside.

3. Pat cucumbers dry with paper towels or a clean kitchen towel. Discard any accumulated liquid in bowl. Transfer cucumbers back into now-empty large bowl.

4. Add carrots, scallions, cilantro, fresno or jalapeño, 1/4 cup peanuts, and half of the reserved peanut-lime dressing to bowl with cucumbers; toss to combine.

5. Transfer cucumber mixture to a large serving bowl or platter. Top with remaining 1/4 cup peanuts and drizzle with remaining dressing. Serve immediately with lime wedges.

6. Box grater, colander

7. 1 1/2 pounds of Persian cucumbers can be used in place of the English cucumbers. There&#39;s no need to peel them as their skins are thin and tender.

8. If the peanut butter has separated, stir it well before making the dressing.

9. The peanut-lime dressing can be made in advance and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

10. The cucumber salad is best eaten right away, while the cucumbers are still crisp.
