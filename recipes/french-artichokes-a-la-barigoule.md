# French Artichokes à la Barigoule

> Artichokes à la barigoule is one of the most famous springtime vegetable dishes from Provence, France. It&#39;s light and bright, with a clean yet comforting flavor, the result of braising artichokes and other vegetables in white wine with olive oil until soft and tender.

## Ingredients

- 4 large or 12 small artichokes ( 2 pounds ; 1 kg )
- 3 tablespoons ( 45 ml ) extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 medium (6-ounce; 170 g ) yellow onion , thinly sliced
- 1 large (5-ounce; 140g) carrot , peeled and cut crosswise into thin rounds
- 3 medium cloves garlic
- Kosher salt
- 1 cup ( 235 ml ) dry white wine
- 3/4 cup ( 175 ml ) homemade chicken stock or low-sodium broth (or vegetable stock , if desired)
- 3 sprigs flat-leaf parsley , 2 sprigs thyme , 1 bay leaf , tied together into a bundle with kitchen twine
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 g ) unsalted butter
- Freshly ground black pepper
- Large pinch minced flat-leaf parsley , for garnish


## Instructions

1. Fill a large bowl with water; halve and squeeze 2 lemons into it.

2. In a 3-quart sauté pan or saucier, heat olive oil over medium heat until shimmering. Add onion, carrot, and garlic, and cook, stirring frequently, until just starting to soften, about 4 minutes. Nestle cleaned artichokes and their stems on top, then add white wine and bring to a simmer; lower heat to maintain a gentle simmer and cook for 4 minutes.

3. Add stock, season lightly with salt, and return to a simmer. Add herbs. Cover and cook at a gentle simmer until artichokes and other vegetables are tender enough to easily slide a fork through them, about 25 minutes (cooking time will vary depending on the age and size of artichokes; if liquid level ever gets too low, add a little more water).

4. Discard herb bundle. Using a slotted spoon, transfer artichokes, onions, carrots, and garlic, to a serving plate and keep warm. Return cooking liquid to the heat and simmer until slightly syrupy and reduced to about 3/4 cup (175ml).

5. Whisk in butter and minced parsley, season with salt and pepper, then pour sauce over warmed platter of vegetables. Serve warm or at room temperature.
