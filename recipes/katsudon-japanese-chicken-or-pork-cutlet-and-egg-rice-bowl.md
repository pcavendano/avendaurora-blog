# Katsudon (Japanese Chicken or Pork Cutlet and Egg Rice Bowl)

> This Japanese katsudon—fried cutlet and egg rice bowl—is the best use you&#39;re ever gonna get out of leftover fried chicken or pork cutlets.

## Ingredients

- 1/3 cup ( 80 ml ) dashi , or 1/3 cup ( 80 ml ) water mixed with 3/4 teaspoon Hondashi
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) sake
- 2 teaspoons ( 8 g ) sugar
- 2 teaspoons ( 10 ml ) mirin
- 4 ounces thinly sliced yellow onion ( 115 g ; about 1/2 medium onion ), optional
- 1 leftover Japanese fried chicken or pork cutlet , cut crosswise into 1/2-inch strips
- 2 large eggs
- 2 scallions , white and light green parts only, thinly sliced, plus more for garnish
- Steamed white or brown rice , for serving


## Instructions

1. Combine dashi, soy sauce, sake, sugar, and mirin in a small saucepan or donburi pan and bring to a simmer over medium heat. If using onion, add to broth and simmer until tender, about 5 minutes. Add sliced fried cutlet and let simmer for 1 minute. Meanwhile, beat together eggs and scallions in a small bowl. Pour egg mixture on top of cutlet and around broth. Cover and cook until eggs are as set as you&#39;d like them, about 1 minute for very soft or 2 minutes for medium. Slide broth, egg, and chicken out on top of a bowl of rice. Sprinkle with scallions and serve.
