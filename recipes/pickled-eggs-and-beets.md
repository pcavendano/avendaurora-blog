# Pickled Eggs and Beets

> Brined in a mixture of cider vinegar, water, pickling spices, and cinnamon, these eggs are sweet and tart, and are ubiquitous at Pennsylvania Dutch picnics and potlucks.

## Ingredients

- 300 g drained sliced pickled beets ( 10 1/2 ounces ; about 2 cups ), liquid reserved (see notes if using fresh beets)
- 6 large eggs, hard boiled and peeled
- 1 1/4 cups ( 300 ml) cider vinegar
- 1/2 cup ( 120 ml) water
- 1 cup granulated sugar ( 7 ounces ; 200 g)
- 1 tablespoon ( 5 g ) pickling spice , see notes
- 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1 (6-inch) cinnamon stick


## Instructions

1. In a 1-quart jar, layer sliced beets and hard-boiled eggs in alternating layers.

2. In a medium saucepan, stir together 1/2 cup (120ml) reserved beet pickling liquid, cider vinegar, water, granulated sugar, pickling spice, salt, and cinnamon stick. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until sugar dissolves, 5 minutes. Reduce heat to low and simmer until brine deepens in color and reduces slightly, about 15 minutes. Remove from heat and discard cinnamon stick.

3. Carefully pour hot brine, along with pickling spices, over beets and eggs to cover completely. Cool to room temperature, about 30 minutes.

4. 1-quart heat-proof jar, medium saucepan

5. The amount of brine will vary depending on the size of your eggs, your chosen container, and how you&#39;ve layered the ingredients. If you find yourself a bit short, top it off with the reserved pickled beet juice.

6. Be careful when transferring your pickled eggs from jar to serving dish, as they will splatter. To prevent beet-stained hands, use a slotted spoon or wear food-grade gloves when handling beet-pickled eggs.

7. If using fresh beets, you’ll need 3 to 4 medium beets (about 12 1/4 ounces; 350g). Trim the ends, then place the unpeeled beets in a large pot of water. Bring to a boil over high heat and cook beets until fork-tender, about 35 minutes. Using a colander or fine-mesh strainer, drain beets, reserving the liquid to use in lieu of the pickled beet juice. Rinse, peel, and slice the beets to 1/4-inch thickness before proceeding with the recipe as written. Refrigerate until beets and eggs are sweet and tangy, at least 7 days.

8. The eggs can be eaten after 24 hours, but will take on more color and flavor the longer they sit. For best results, allow eggs to pickle for at least 3 days.

9. This is a quick pickling recipe, and is not intended for canning or long-term preservation. Store pickled eggs in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
