# Crispy, Oven-Baked Sweet Potato Fries

> Learn the secret to perfectly crispy oven-baked sweet potato fries using a cornstarch trick—no deep frying needed.

## Ingredients

- Cooking spray
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 ml ) neutral oil , such as vegetable oil
- 3/4 cup ( 180 ml ) water
- 3 tablespoons cornstarch
- 3 tablespoons white rice flour
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 2 (12 ounces each) sweet potatoes , trimmed, cut in half crosswise, and cut into 1/2-inch thick sticks
- 1 1/2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt , plus extra for seasoning; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1/2 teaspoon sweet paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1 tablespoon ketchup
- 1 tablespoon sriracha
- 2 teaspoons white wine vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder


## Instructions

1. Adjust oven rack to lowest position and heat oven to 425°F (220°C). Spray rimmed baking sheet with cooking spray. Add oil to prepared sheet and tilt sheet until surface is evenly coated with oil. In a small bowl, combine salt, paprika, garlic powder, and cayenne; set aside.

2. In a large microwave-safe bowl, whisk water and cornstarch, making sure no lumps of cornstarch remain on bottom of bowl. Microwave, stirring every 20 seconds, until mixture begins to thicken, 1 to 3 minutes. Remove from microwave and continue stirring until mixture thickens to a thin yogurt-like consistency. (If necessary, after microwaving, add up to 3 tablespoons additional water to achieve correct consistency.)

3. Bake until bottoms are beginning to brown and fries easily release from pan, about 20 minutes. Remove sheet from oven and, using a thin spatula, loosen the fries and carefully flip taking care not to tear. Rotate pan and return to oven to continue to bake until second sides are golden brown, about 15 minutes.

4. Sprinkle fries with prepared salt mixture and toss fries to distribute seasoning evenly. Transfer fries to paper towel–lined platter and let cool about 5 minutes. Serve fries with prepared sauce, if desired.

5. Rimmed baking sheet, thin metal spatula, paper towels

6. Homemade seasoned salt can be substituted with 1 1/2 teaspoons of your favorite store-bought seasoned salt.

7. The seasoned salt can be made ahead and stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 month.

8. The fry sauce can be made ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 7 days.
