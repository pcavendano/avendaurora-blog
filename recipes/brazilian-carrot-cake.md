# Brazilian Carrot Cake

> Bolo de cenoura is a Brazilian carrot cake that’s balanced, just sweet enough without being cloying, and equally wonderful as a snacking cake or as a dessert for a special occasion.

## Ingredients

- Baking spray with flour
- 3 medium carrots ( 4 ounces ; 113 g each), peeled and grated (about 2 cups packed)
- 14 ounces granulated sugar ( 400 g ; 2 cups )
- 1 cup ( 240 ml ) vegetable oil
- 3 large eggs
- 8 1/2 ounces all-purpose flour ( 240 g ; 1 3/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons )
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 2 (3 1/2 ounce) cans sweetened condensed milk
- 3/4 cup ( 180 ml ) whole milk
- 1 1/2 ounces unsweetened natural cocoa powder ( 42 g ; 1/2 cup )
- 1 ounce unsalted butter ( 28 g ; 2 tablespoons ), cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1/4 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- Chocolate sprinkles (optional)


## Instructions

1. For the Cake: Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Using baking spray, lightly grease bottom and sides of a 9-inch round cake pan; line bottom of pan with parchment paper.

2. In a blender or food processor, blend carrots, sugar, oil, and eggs until completely smooth, about 90 seconds. In a large bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, and salt together. Pour carrot mixture into flour mixture and whisk until combined and no lumps remain. Pour batter into prepared pan and gently tap to eliminate any air bubbles. Using an offset or flexible spatula, smooth batter into an even layer.

3. Bake until golden brown and a toothpick or cake tester inserted comes out clean, 45 to 50 minutes. Tent with aluminum foil, if needed, after 30 minutes to prevent overbrowning. Let cake cool in pan 15 minutes; invert cake onto a wire rack and let cool completely, about 1 hour.

4. For the Frosting : In a small saucepan, whisk condensed milk, whole milk, cocoa powder, butter, and salt to combine. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture begins to bubble and has thickened slightly, about 5 minutes.

5. Immediately pour frosting over cooled cake, allowing it to drip down sides of cake. Garnish with chocolate sprinkles, if desired. Let frosting set before slicing and serving, at least 30 minutes.

6. 9-inch round cake pan, parchment paper, blender or food processor, whisk, toothpick or cake tester, wire rack, small saucepan

7. Cake can be baked in a Bundt pan or a 9- by 13-inch baking pan if desired. Baking time may vary; if baking in a 9- by 13-inch baking pan, bake until golden brown and a toothpick or cake tester inserted comes out clean, about 30 minutes.

8. Cake can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days.

9. The cooled and unfrosted cake can be wrapped tight in plastic and frozen for up to 2 months. Let thaw in refrigerator overnight before frosting.
