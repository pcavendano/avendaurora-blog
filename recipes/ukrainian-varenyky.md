# Ukrainian Varenyky

> Tossed in butter and served with dollops of sour cream and plenty of fresh dill, these plump cheese-filled varenyky are beloved throughout Ukraine.

## Ingredients

- 350g ( 12 1/2 ounces ) cottage cheese
- 1 large egg yolk
- 1/4 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 285 g all-purpose flour (10 ounces; about 2 1/4 cups), divided, plus more for dusting
- 1/2 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 56 g unsalted butter (about 2 ounces; 4 tablespoons), cut into 1-inch cubes, and softened to 65ºF or 18ºC
- 1 large egg , lightly beaten
- 1 tablespoon (15ml) sour cream
- 1/3 cup (70ml) water , room temperature
- 2 tablespoons (20g) Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 28 g unsalted butter (about 1 ounce; 2 tablespoons), softened and cut into 4 pieces
- Sour cream or crème fraîche, for serving
- Small handful chopped dill , for serving (optional)


## Instructions

1. For the Filling: Line a fine-mesh strainer with 4 layers of cheesecloth and set in a large mixing bowl. Place cottage cheese on cheesecloth and fold over and cover with overhanging cheesecloth. Place a heavy weight, such as a 28-ounce can tomatoes, on top. Refrigerate until cheese is slightly crumbly and no moisture remains, at least 24 hours or up to 48 hours.

2. Using a wooden spoon or flexible spatula, press strained cottage cheese through a fine-mesh strainer set over a small bowl. In a separate small bowl, combine 300g (10 1/2 ounces) of the strained cottage cheese with egg yolk and salt. Using a flexible spatula, mix until no streaks of yolk remain. Refrigerate until chilled and firm, at least 30 minutes.

3. To Fill the Dumplings: Place half a tablespoon (11g) of filling in the center of each dough round. Working with one dough round at a time, fold dough over the filling to form a half-moon while distributing filling to fit within it, and firmly pinch edges to seal. Transfer the cheese dumplings to prepared baking sheet. Repeat with remaining dough and filling. You should have 25 dumplings.

4. To Cook the Dumplings: In a 6-quart pot, bring 4 quarts (3.8 liters) of water to a boil. Salt boiling water with 2 tablespoons (about 3/4 ounces; 20g) kosher salt. Carefully add half of the dumplings to boiling water, gently stirring with a spatula or wooden spoon to prevent the dumplings from sticking to the bottom of the pot, and cook until dumplings float, about 2 minutes. Once floating, cook for 1 more minute to ensure the dough and filling are heated through. Using a spider skimmer, transfer the varenyky, shaking off excess water as needed, to a large bowl with 2 tablespoons (28g) butter. Gently toss to evenly coat varenyky. Repeat with remaining dumplings.

5. Serve cheese varenyk with a dollop of cold sour cream and chopped dill.

6. The fillings are easier to use cold and should be used straight out of the fridge. Do not let them warm up before using.

7. The recipe can easily be doubled if desired.

8. To prevent the dumplings from drying out as you fill and shape them, keep shaped varenyky covered with plastic wrap or a lightly damped kitchen towel so they don’t dry out.

9. Dressing varenyky in butter will prevent the dumplings from sticking and drying out.

10. The cottage cheese can be strained to 2 days in advance and refrigerated in an airtight container. The cottage cheese filling can be made up to 24 hours in advance and refrigerated in an airtight container.

11. Shaped, uncooked varenyky can be frozen for up to 1 month. To freeze dumplings, place shaped varenyky on a rimmed baking sheet lined with parchment. Wrap baking sheet tightly with plastic. When dumplings are frozen, transfer them to a zip-top bag or airtight container.
