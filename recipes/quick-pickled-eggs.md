# Quick-Pickled Eggs

> These easy quick-pickled eggs are sweet, savory, and have just enough kick from black peppercorns. Plus: They require just 20 minutes of active time.

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs , hard-boiled and peeled
- 6 (4-inch) sprigs fresh dill ( 1/4 ounce ; 7 g )
- 3 medium cloves garlic ( 15 g ), peeled
- 2 cups ( 480 ml ) distilled white vinegar
- 1 cup ( 240 ml ) water
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar ( 3 1/2 ounces ; 100 g )
- 3 dried bay leaves
- 2 teaspoons peppercorns
- 2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume


## Instructions

1. In a 1-quart heat-safe glass jar with a lid, combine hard-boiled eggs with dill and garlic.

2. In a medium saucepan, stir together vinegar, water, sugar, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Cook over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until sugar dissolves, 5 minutes. Reduce heat to low and simmer until liquid reduces and darkens slightly, 10 minutes.

3. Carefully pour hot brine, along with bay leaves and peppercorns, over eggs, garlic, and dill. Cool to room temperature, about 1 hour.

4. Cover with a lid and refrigerate until eggs taste and smell vinegary and become beige, for at least 3 days and up to 2 weeks. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

5. To make onion-pickled eggs: Peel a small (approximately 130g; 4 1/2 ounce) white onion. Slice into thin rings. Layer with the hard-boiled eggs, then proceed with the recipe as written.

6. You can make quick-pickled eggs in any lidded glass jar or heat-proof food storage container.

7. 8 large hard-boiled eggs fit comfortably in a 1-quart jar, but if you are using smaller eggs, you might have room for 1 to 2 more. Avoid cramming too many eggs into the jar to provide adequate space for the brine to fully surround the eggs.

8. The recipe can easily be doubled. To pickle 16 eggs, use a 2-quart heat-proof jar or two 1-quart containers.

9. You can eat the eggs after 24 hours, but for maximum flavor, refrigerate for at least 3 days before serving.

10. This is a quick pickling recipe, and is not intended for canning or long-term preservation. Store quick-pickled eggs in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.
