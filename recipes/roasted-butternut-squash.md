# Roasted Butternut Squash

> Butternut squash is sweet and starchy, and its versatility makes the ingredient a favorite among many home cooks. Here&#39;s how to roast squash that&#39;s creamy and tender every time.

## Ingredients

- 1 pound ( 453 g ) butternut squash , peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch cubes
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 ml ) extra-virgin olive oil or other cooking fat (see note)
- 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt , plus more as needed; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (optional, see note)


## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 400ºF (200ºC). Toss squash with olive oil, salt, and baking soda (if using), and arrange on a rimmed baking sheet, making sure not to overcrowd the sheet. Roast, using a thin metal spatula to turn pieces 2 or 3 times during cooking, until squash is tender and golden brown, about 25 minutes. Set aside.

2. If using baking soda, watch the squash carefully as it cooks since baking soda acts as an accelerant for browning and the sugars can easily burn. Baking soda also helps to soften and break down pectin, a fiber that contributes to the firm texture of squash, which is helpful when mashing or puréeing the squash after. If you’re looking for squash with more bite, skip the baking soda.
