## Instructions

1. In a 12-inch stainless-steel skillet, combine sugar, water, orange juice, and vanilla and cook over medium-high heat until simmering and sugar is dissolved.

2. Add cherries along with a pinch of salt and orange zest (if using), cover, and cook until cherries are softened and have released their juices, about 4 minutes. Uncover skillet and continue to cook, stirring occasionally, until cherries are very tender and caramel has thickened to a glaze, about 4 minutes longer.

3. Add kirsch (do not pour directly from the bottle if working over an open flame), then tilt pan to ignite the alcohol from a gas burner, or carefully light with a match. Cook, shaking the pan, until the flames have died out and the caramel has thickened to a glaze, about 1 to 2 minutes. Alternatively, you can add the kirsch and not light it on fire, instead simmering until the alcohol has mostly cooked off and the caramel has reached a glaze consistency.

4. Add vinegar, starting with 1 teaspoon and adding more to taste, if desired, to balance sweetness of sauce. Stir cornstarch slurry to recombine, then add, 1 teaspoon at a time and bringing to a boil between additions, until sauce forms a thickened, honey-like consistency, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then serve with ice cream.

5. In a 12-inch stainless-steel skillet, combine sugar, reserved cherry juices, orange juice, and vanilla and cook over medium-high heat until simmering and sugar is dissolved.

6. Add drained cherries along with a pinch of salt and orange zest (if using) and cook, stirring occasionally, until cherries are very tender and caramel has thickened to a lightly syrupy glaze, about 3 minutes.

7. Add kirsch (do not pour directly from the bottle if working over an open flame), then tilt pan to ignite the alcohol from a gas burner, or carefully light with a match. Cook, shaking the pan, until the flames have died out and the caramel has thickened to a glaze, about 1 to 2 minutes. Alternatively, you can add the kirsch and not light it on fire, instead simmering until the alcohol has mostly cooked off and the caramel has reached a glaze consistency.

8. Add vinegar, starting with 1 teaspoon and adding more to taste, if desired, to balance sweetness of sauce. Stir cornstarch slurry to recombine, then add, 1 teaspoon at a time and bringing to a boil between additions, until sauce forms a thickened, honey-like consistency, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then serve with ice cream.

9. In a 12-inch stainless-steel skillet, combine sugar, reserved cherry juices, orange juice, and vanilla and cook over medium-high heat until simmering and sugar is dissolved.

10. Add drained cherries along with a pinch of salt and orange zest (if using) and cook, stirring occasionally, until cherries are very tender and caramel has thickened to a lightly syrupy glaze, about 3 minutes.

11. Add kirsch (do not pour directly from the bottle if working over an open flame), then tilt pan to ignite the alcohol from a gas burner, or carefully light with a match. Cook, shaking the pan, until the flames have died out and the caramel has thickened to a glaze, about 1 to 2 minutes. Alternatively, you can add the kirsch and not light it on fire, instead simmering until the alcohol has mostly cooked off and the caramel has reached a glaze consistency.

12. Stir cornstarch slurry to recombine, then add, 1 teaspoon at a time and bringing to a boil between additions, until sauce forms a thickened, honey-like consistency, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then serve with ice cream.

13. In a 12-inch stainless-steel skillet, combine sugar, reserved cherry syrup, orange juice, and vanilla and cook over medium-high heat until simmering and sugar is dissolved.

14. Add drained cherries along with a pinch of salt and orange zest (if using) and cook, stirring occasionally, until cherries are very tender and caramel has thickened to a lightly syrupy glaze, about 3 minutes.

15. Add kirsch (do not pour directly from the bottle if working over an open flame), then tilt pan to ignite the alcohol from a gas burner, or carefully light with a match. Cook, shaking the pan, until the flames have died out and the caramel has thickened to a glaze, about 1 to 2 minutes. Alternatively, you can add the kirsch and not light it on fire, instead simmering until the alcohol has mostly cooked off and the caramel has reached a glaze consistency.

16. Stir cornstarch slurry to recombine, then add, 1 teaspoon at a time and bringing to a boil between additions, until sauce forms a thickened, honey-like consistency, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then serve with ice cream.

17. The cherry sauce can be made up to 2 days in advance and refrigerated in an airtight container. Reheat gently; if syrup has become too thick, thin with water if necessary, 1 teaspoon at a time until returned to original consistency. Serve warm over ice cream.
