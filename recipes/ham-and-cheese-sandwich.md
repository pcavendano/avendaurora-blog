# Ham and Cheese Sandwich

> For the perfect ham and cheese sandwich, layer ribbons of satiny ham with tangy Swiss cheese, mustard, and pickles, then cook it until the bread is golden and the melted cheese blankets the pork.

## Ingredients

- Four 1/2-inch-thick slices crusty bread or hearty sandwich bread (see notes)
- 2 tablespoons mayonnaise or softened unsalted butter, divided
- 2 teaspoons ( 10 g ) Dijon mustard , divided
- 8 thin slices ounces Swiss , Gruyère , or cheddar cheese (about 4 ounces ; 115 g ), divided
- 6 ounces ( 170 g ) thinly sliced ham (about 12 slices ), divided
- 8 to 12 pickles chips , optional


## Instructions

1. Place bread slices on a clean work surface. Using a butter knife, spread 1 teaspoon mayonnaise or butter evenly over 1 side of each slice. Place 2 slices (mayonnaise side down) into a 12-inch nonstick skillet. Spread the top (dry side) of the 2 bread slices in skillet with the remaining mayonnaise or butter.

2. Place 2 slices (about 1 ounce) of the cheese in an even layer on each bread slice in skillet. (Cut and/or layer cheese if needed to avoid cheese draping off the sides of the bread.) Top with the ham and pickle chips (if using), divided evenly between the 2 slices. Top with the remaining cheese ( 2 slices per sandwich). Spread 1 teaspoon mayonnaise evenly over the dry side of the remaining 2 bread slices, then spread with mustard. Close each sandwich with the remaining bread slices, mustard side down. Using a spatula, press firmly to adhere.

3. Heat skillet over medium heat and cook until bread is golden brown on bottom, 4 to 6 minutes. Using 2 spatulas, flip sandwiches. Set an empty large Dutch oven or other heavy skillet or cooking weight directly on top of sandwiches, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook until second side is golden brown and cheese is melted, 2 to 4 minutes.

4. Using a spatula, transfer sandwiches to a wire rack and let sit for 5 minutes. Transfer sandwiches to a cutting board, cut in half and serve warm.

5. 12-inch nonstick skillet, large Dutch oven or other heavy skillet or cooking weight

6. I like to use rustic artisanal bread for this recipe; avoid using baguette, but rather look for a wide loaf that will yield flat slices for direct contact with the skillet.

7. Feel free to customize this sandwich any way you’d like. Here are a few examples.

8. Serve it untoasted: The sandwich can be made cold and untoasted. Use the same ingredients and layer the ingredients within the sandwich in the same order, just omit spreading mayonnaise on the exterior of the sandwich.

9. Swap in butter for mayonnaise: While I’ve sung mayo’s praises above as the fat of choice with this ham and cheese, don’t feel tied to using it. You can swap in softened butter for the mayo in this recipe, both as the condiment and the cooking medium.

10. Omit the pickles: If you don’t like pickles, don’t use them. It’s that simple.

11. Add your preferred fillings: You can use this technique and ratio of cheese to meat as a starting point and swap in your preferred deli meat or use a combo of meats. I’d still recommend using thinly sliced meat. Or add some heat to the sandwich with sliced pickled banana peppers or even some chili crisp. Have fun with it.
