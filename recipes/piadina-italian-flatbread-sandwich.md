# Piadina (Italian Flatbread Sandwich)

> To make a stellar piadiana, fold a freshly griddled flatbread over a heap of peppery arugula and creamy stracchino or burrata. Customize it further with your favorite fillings—just be sure to not overload it.

## Ingredients

- 1 3/4 cups ( 245 g ) all-purpose flour
- 3/4 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; if using table salt use half as much by volume
- 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
- 3 tablespoons ( 40 g ) lard or shortening
- 1/2 cup ( 355 ml ) whole milk , warm
- Olive oil , for skillet
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon ( 15 ml ) balsamic vinegar
- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 cup stracchino or stracciatella cheese ( 8 ounces ; 225 g ), or 2 ( 4-ounce ) balls burrata, halved
- 4 cups arugula ( 4 ounces ; 115 g )


## Instructions

1. For the Dough: Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 250℉ (120℃). In a medium bowl, thoroughly whisk together flour, salt, and baking soda. Use your finger to pinch the lard into the flour mixture until lard is broken into pea-sized or smaller pieces and coated with flour. Add milk and stir until a shaggy dough forms. Transfer dough to a clean work surface and knead until smooth, 3 to 5 minutes. Shape into a ball, cover dough loosely with plastic wrap and let rest for 20 minutes.

2. Divide dough into 4 equal pieces and shape each into a ball. Cover dough balls loosely with plastic wrap and let rest for 20 minutes.

3. On a lightly floured work surface, pat 1 dough ball into a 5-inch disk (keep remaining dough balls covered). Using a rolling pin, roll disk into a 10-inch circle, flouring counter and rolling pin as needed to prevent sticking (dough rounds should be ¼ inch thick—about the thickness of a piece of cardstock). Use a fork to lightly prick the dough rounds all over on just 1 side, making sure to prick all the way through to the other side (about 8 times); set aside and cover. Repeat with remaining dough balls.

4. Preheat an empty 12-inch stainless-steel or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat until very hot (a drop of water dripped onto the surface should sizzle immediately), 2 to 4 minutes. Using a pastry brush, coat the hot skillet lightly with oil. Place 1 dough round into skillet and cook until the bottom side is spotty brown and bubbles form across the dough’s top surface, about 30 seconds. Using a spatula, flip dough and continue to cook until dough puffs and the second side is spotty brown, about 30 seconds. Use a spatula to transfer piadina to a rimmed baking sheet and hold warm in the oven while cooking the remaining piadine. Repeat with the remaining 3 dough rounds.

5. For the Filling: In a large bowl, toss arugula with olive oil and balsamic vinegar and season with salt and pepper to taste; set aside.

6. Scatter 1 cup dressed arugula evenly over the cheese. Fold piadina in half (over the greens) and press gently to secure shut. Repeat steps 6 and 7 with the remaining 3 piadine. Slice in half and serve.

7. Rolling pin, 12-inch stainless-steel skillet, pastry brush, rimmed baking sheet

8. Once cooled, the cooked flatbread can be wrapped tightly with plastic wrap and stored at room temperature for up to 1 day. Reheat briefly in a skillet to warm through before building the sandwich.
