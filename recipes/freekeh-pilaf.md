# Freekeh Pilaf

> For a simple pilaf that accentuates freekeh&#39;s unique chew and smoky flavor, it’s critical to soak the grains before cooking and pair it with warm spices and aromatics.

## Ingredients

- 1 cup cracked freekeh (6 ounces; 170 g) , picked over and rinsed
- 3 tablespoons (45 ml) ghee, butter and/or extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1/2 medium yellow onion (4 ounces; 112 g), cut into 1/2-inch thick pieces
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 3 green cardamom pods
- 2 cups (480ml) homemade chicken stock or store bought low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
- 1 1/4 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt use half as much by volume
- Lemon wedges for serving
- Plain yogurt for serving , optional


## Instructions

1. In a large bowl, cover freekeh with at least 1 inch of room temperature water and let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes. Use a fine-mesh strainer to drain well.

2. In a medium saucepan, heat 2 1/2 tablespoons oil or ghee over medium heat until shimmering. Add onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they&#39;re just starting to soften, about 3 minutes. Add freekeh and toast, stirring frequently, until freekeh is fragrant, starts to stick to bottom of pan, and makes a crackling popping sound, about 3 minutes.

3. Stir in cinnamon, nutmeg, black pepper, and cardamom, and cook, stirring constantly, until spices are fragrant, about 30 seconds.

4. Stir in broth and salt, scraping bottom of pan, and bring to a simmer. Cover, and cook over medium-low heat, adjusting heat as needed to maintain a gentle simmer and stirring occasionally while making sure to scrape bottom of pan, until freekeh is tender yet slightly chewy, 18 to 20 minutes. Off-heat, let freekeh sit, covered, for 10 minutes. Add remaining 1/2 tablespoon oil or ghee and fluff with fork to combine, removing cardamom pods, if desired. Season with salt to taste. Serve with lemon wedges and yogurt, if desired.

5. Medium saucepan and fine-mesh strainer

6. Ghee can be purchased from Middle Eastern, Indian, and South Asian grocery stores. It is also increasingly found in the international aisle or cooking oil aisle of American grocery stores.

7. Cracked freekeh can be purchased from Middle Eastern grocery stores, some supermarkets, and online.

8. Leftover freekeh can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days.
