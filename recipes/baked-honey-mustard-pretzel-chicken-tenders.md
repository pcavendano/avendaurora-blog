# Baked Honey-Mustard Pretzel Chicken Tenders

> These easy chicken tenders are seasoned with homemade honey-mustard, breaded in salty pretzel crumbs, then dipped in a tangy mustard sauce. They’re great baked in the oven or in an air fryer.

## Ingredients

- Cooking spray
- 1 1/2 pounds ( 680 g ) chicken tenderloins (see notes)
- 2 teaspoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt , divided; for table salt use half as much by volume
- 1/2 cup ( 2.25 ounces ; 64 g ) all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup ( 120 ml ) yellow mustard
- 1/2 cup ( 120 ml ) mayonnaise
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) honey
- 7 ounces ( 200 g ) thin pretzel sticks or thin mini twists
- 1/4 cup ( 60 ml ) extra-virgin olive oil


## Instructions

1. For the Chicken: Pat chicken tenders dry with paper towels and season evenly with 1 1/2 teaspoons salt. Set aside while you prepare the dredge.

2. Spread flour in a shallow dish. In a second shallow dish, whisk mustard, mayonnaise, honey, and remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt until well combined. Transfer 1/2 cup sauce to a small serving bowl, cover, and set aside in refrigerator.

3. Add pretzels to the bowl of a food processor. Process until mostly finely ground with some larger pieces remaining, about 20 seconds. (Alternatively, add pretzels to a large, heavy-duty resealable bag, press out the air, seal, and use a rolling pin to crush pretzels into fine crumbs. Note that some pretzels may break through the bag.)

4. Transfer pretzel crumbs to a third shallow dish (you should have about 2 cups). Add oil and use fingers to toss until crumbs are evenly coated in oil.

5. Working with one chicken tender at a time, dredge in flour, shaking off excess. Dip chicken in honey-mustard sauce, let excess drip off, then dredge in pretzel crumbs, pressing gently to help crumbs adhere; transfer chicken to greased rack set in baking sheet. Repeat the process with remaining tenders, then discard remaining flour, sauce, and crumbs.

6. For Cooking in the Oven: Bake tenders until golden brown, pretzel coating is crunchy, and internal temperature reaches 160°F (75°C), 20 to 25 minutes. Transfer chicken to a serving platter and let rest for 5 minutes.

7. Rimmed baking sheet, wire rack, aluminum foil or parchment paper, food processor or large resealable bag and rolling pin, instant-read thermometer

8. You can use pre-prepped chicken tenders (labeled as tenderloins or tenders) or make your own tenders from boneless, skinless chicken breasts. To do so, first remove the tenderloins from the breasts, then cut the breasts lengthwise into 2 to 3 strips of similar thickness (about 2 to 3 ounces; 57 to 85 g each). You should end up with about 8 pieces of chicken.

9. Make sure to use thin pretzel sticks or thin mini twists, not thick pretzel rods or larger sourdough pretzels, in this recipe.

10. The honey-mustard mayonnaise can be made ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
