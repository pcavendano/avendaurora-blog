# Honey-Butter Toast

> This riff on brick toast is fluffy and soft on the inside with a shatteringly crisp honey-butter exterior. The recipe comes together quickly and then bakes in the oven, so this luxurious breakfast or snack is almost entirely hands off.

## Ingredients

- 2 tablespoons ( 30 g ) unsalted butter , softened
- 2 tablespoons ( 30 g ) light brown sugar
- 2 1/2 teaspoons ( 15 g ) honey , divided, plus more for drizzling
- 1/8 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt ; for table salt, use half as much by volume
- 1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/8 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional)
- 1 2-inch thick slice brioche , challah , milk bread , or soft white bread ( 3 ounces ; 90 g )
- 1/4 cup ( 113 g ) strained Greek-style yogurt
- 1/4 teaspoon vanilla bean paste
- Fresh mixed berries , for serving


## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Line a small rimmed baking sheet with parchment. In a small bowl, stir together butter, brown sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons of the honey, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom (if using) until smooth.

2. If desired, trim crusts from bread to yield a perfectly square slice. Spread a thin layer of butter mixture over top, bottom, and sides of each bread slice. Place on prepared baking sheet.

3. Bake until very lightly golden-brown, 12 to 16 minutes. Flip slices and continue to bake until evenly golden, 5 to 7 minutes longer.

4. Remove from oven and cool on baking sheet for 5 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack and cool until crispy, about 10 minutes.

5. While toast cools, whisk together yogurt and vanilla bean paste until smooth. Stir in remaining 1 teaspoon honey, maintaining a swirl by not completely stirring it in.

6. Place crisped toast on a plate; dollop with yogurt, then spoon berries on top. Drizzle with more honey, if desired. Serve immediately.

7. Small rimmed baking sheet, parchment

8. The butter mixture can be made up to 3 days ahead and stored at room temperature in an airtight container or refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 weeks. Let it come to room temperature before use for easier spreadability.

9. Bread can be slathered with the butter mixture and then refrigerated overnight for easier baking in the morning.
