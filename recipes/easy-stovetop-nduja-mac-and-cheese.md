# Easy Stovetop 'Nduja Mac and Cheese

> Gooey, spicy, meaty mac and cheese—all it takes is four pantry-friendly ingredients and 10 minutes of your time.

## Ingredients

- 6 ounces ( 170 g ) elbow macaroni
- Kosher salt
- 6 ounces ( 180 ml ) evaporated milk
- 6 ounces ( 170 g ) grated mild or medium cheddar cheese , or any good melting cheese, such as Fontina, Gruyère, or Jack
- 2 ounces ( 1/4 cup ; 56 g ) &#39;nduja (see notes)


## Instructions

1. Place macaroni in a medium saucepan and add just enough cold water to cover. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a boil over high heat, stirring frequently with a heat-resistant flexible spatula to prevent pasta from sticking. Continue to cook, stirring frequently, until water has been almost completely absorbed and macaroni is just shy of al dente, about 6 minutes.

2. Immediately add evaporated milk, stir to combine, and bring to a boil. Add cheese, stir to incorporate, then add &#39;nduja, continuing to stir to combine.

3. Reduce heat to low and cook, stirring continuously, until cheese is melted, &#39;nduja is fully emulsified, and liquid has reduced to a creamy sauce, about 2 minutes longer. Taste for seasoning, adding more salt if necessary (&#39;nduja is quite salty, so pasta shouldn&#39;t need any additional salt). Serve immediately.

4. 3-quart saucier or medium saucepan, heat-proof flexible spatula

5. 'Nduja is a spicy spreadable pork salume that originates from the southern Italian region of Calabria. In the US, it can be purchased at specialty shops that carry salumi/charcuterie, Italian markets, or online from producers like Tempesta Artisan Salumi . Look for 'nduja made with just four ingredients: pork, Calabrian chiles, salt, and lactic acid.

6. For a milder and less &#39;nduja-forward mac and cheese, or if you&#39;re looking to make your &#39;nduja supply last as long as possible, reduce the amount of &#39;nduja by half, and use just 1 ounce (2 tablespoons; 28g).

7. Macaroni and cheese is best enjoyed immediately.
