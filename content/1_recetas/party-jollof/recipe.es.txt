  step_title: ""
  instruction: In a 3-quart saucier or saucepan, add blended vegetable base and bring to a boil over medium-high heat, cover partially. Once boiling, reduce heat to medium-low and cook, stirring occasionally and scraping bottom of pan with a wooden spoon to avoid scorching, until reduced by half (roughly about 2 1/2 cups; 590 ml), about 30 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Jollof Rice: In a 4- or 5-quart pot or Dutch oven, heat 2 tablespoons oil over medium heat until shimmering. Add half of onion, bay leaves, curry powder, dried thyme, a large pinch of salt, and a large pinch of black or white pepper. Cook, stirring constantly, until mixture is fragrant and onion softens slightly, about 3 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Stir in stock and bring to a boil over high heat. Season with salt and pepper to taste. The curry powder should come through pleasantly but not be overpowering; whisk in more curry powder to taste if desired.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Stir in rice until evenly coated in sauce. Cover pot with a double piece of aluminum foil or parchment paper, crimped down around the edges to seal pot tightly, then top with lid. Reduce heat to the lowest setting and cook for 20 minutes. Uncover pot and gently stir rice to redistribute. Cover again and continue to cook until rice is just cooked through but grains still retain a firm bite and the liquid is mostly absorbed, about 15 minutes longer. If rice is undercooked and/or still wet, cover and cook 5 minutes longer.
  tip: ""
