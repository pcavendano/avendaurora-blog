Instructions:
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Yogurt Sauce: In a medium bowl, whisk together yogurt, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, parsley, and salt. Season to taste with more salt if needed. Cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Squash: Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Halve the butternut squash lengthwise, then use a large spoon or ice cream scoop to scoop out all the seeds. With a paring knife, score the flesh in a shallow crosshatch.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, salt, cumin, and paprika, then brush or rub over squash flesh to coat evenly.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Transfer squash cut-side up on a rimmed baking sheet and roast until flesh is tender and lightly caramelized, 45 to 55 minutes. If edges brown too quickly while roasting, tent loosely with foil.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Kefta and Couscous: While the squash is roasting, in a large skillet, heat oil over medium heat until shimmering. Add onion and cook until softened, stirring occasionally, about 5 minutes. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper, and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add ground meat and cook, breaking up the meat with a wooden spoon and stirring frequently until fully cooked, about 8 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Stir in the cooked couscous, cilantro, parsley, raisins, and almonds. Season to taste with salt.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: To Serve: Spread yogurt sauce onto a large serving plate, place the squash halves on top, and spoon the kefta mixture inside the cavities of the squash and spoon any remaining mixture on top of and around the squash on the plate. Serve immediately.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Rimmed baking sheet, large skillet
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: The yogurt sauce can be made up to 7 days ahead and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
  tip: ""
  step_image:

Tips:

- The ketfa and couscous mixture can be prepared up to 4 days ahead and stored in an airtightcontainer in the refrigerator. Reheat in a skillet before serving.
