Tips:

- Any size shrimp can be used in this recipe. I prefer to use small or extra small shrimp left whole, but if using medium, large, or larger shrimp, chopping the shrimp into bite-size pieces is important for easier eating.
- Manchego, asadero, and Chihuahua cheeses are all commonly used in Sinaloa. Out of those three, I prefer manchego, a mild sheep’s milk cheese—it has a little bit of a sharp edge that works really well with the shrimp. If none of the three recommended cheeses are available, I recommend Gouda or Monterrey Jack because they&#39;re very similar to queso Chihuahua and widely available in the US.
- The stewed shrimp can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Gently reheat in a skillet before assembling the tacos.
