Instructions:
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Wearing latex gloves, prepare Scotch bonnet peppers; preparation will depend on your heat tolerance. For very spicy heat, stem and chop or slice peppers, keeping seeds and white pith; for medium heat, stem and seed peppers, then chop or slice; for mild heat, leave peppers whole, including stems.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: In a medium bowl, add shrimp with Scotch bonnets, bell pepper, onion, thyme, garlic, annatto oil (or neutral oil), salt, annatto powder, garlic powder, and onion powder. Stir to combine well. Cover with plastic and refrigerate at least 30 minutes and up to overnight.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Using a gloved hand, scrape chiles, vegetables, and herbs off shrimp and set shrimp aside in a clean bowl. Add water to vegetable marinade, then pour mixture into a stainless-steel skillet large enough to hold shrimp in a single layer.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Bring to a simmer over medium heat, add allspice berries, then cover and cook at a simmer for 15 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Add vinegar, turn heat to medium-low, and add shrimp to the skillet in a single layer. Cover and cook for 2 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Turn off heat, uncover, and stir until shrimp are just cooked through and no longer translucent, about 2 minutes longer. Season with additional salt, if desired.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Transfer shrimp to a serving plate and let cool slightly. Discard cooking liquid and vegetables. Serve shrimp warm or at room temperature, peeling them at the table.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: The shrimp can be refrigerated in their marinade for up to 12 hours before cooking.
