Description: Tender naan with a crackling, thin, crisp crust is even better when studded with garlic and glazed with an aromatic garlic-butter mixture before serving. It complements most curries and soups and almost any meal.

Tips:

- Once all naan are all baked, use a pastry brush to coat top of each warm naan with the prepared garlic butter and transfer to a serving platter or basket. Sprinkle naan with salt to taste and with cilantro. Serve immediately.
- Rolling pin, baking stone or rimmed baking sheet, 2 clean dry kitchen towels, pastry brush
- The prepared and kneaded dough (through step 3) can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 24 hours. When ready to make naan, bring the dough to room temperature for 10 minutes before shaping. Then continue from step 4.
- Baked naan can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 day.
