Tips:

- 12-inch cast iron, carbon steel, or stainless-steel skillet, large Dutch oven, spider skimmer
- An equivalent weight of porterhouse, T-bone, tenderloin, hanger, or flank steak can be used in place of ribeye steak. Try to get steaks at least one-and-a-half inches thick. If using a thinner cut of steak, shorten cooking time accordingly.
- Make sure to use European-style or French-style green peppercorns and not Thai-style green peppercorns as the flavor of the brine is slightly different.
- The sauce is best enjoyed right away, but it can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Gently reheat in a saucepan over low heat on the stove top, whisking constantly before serving.
