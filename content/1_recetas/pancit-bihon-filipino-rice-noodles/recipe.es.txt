Title: Pancit Bihon (Filipino Rice Noodles)

----

Category: platos-fuertes, sopas-caldos

----

Description: Stir-fried rice vermicelli with carrots, snow peas, and shredded chicken comprise one of the most common pancit iterations you’ll find on the archipelago. This version simmers chicken thighs with water to yield both shredded chicken and stock before stir-frying.

Instructions:
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Chicken: Fill a 4-quart saucepan halfway with water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Add chicken thighs, return to a boil, partially cover with lid, then lower heat to maintain a simmer. Cook, until chicken is tender and falling away from the bone, about 40 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Using tongs, transfer chicken thighs to a rimmed baking sheet or platter and set aside until cool enough to handle, about 10 minutes. Strain remaining liquid through a fine-mesh strainer set over a large heatproof bowl; set aside (you should have about 1 1/2 quarts; 1.4L). Once chicken is cool, finely shred chicken using two forks; discard skin and bones. Set aside.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Pancit Noodles: In a wok, heat oil over high heat until shimmering. Add garlic and onion and cook, stirring frequently, until softened and light brown in color, about 2 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Add carrots and snow peas and cook, stirring frequently, until vegetables have brightened in color and are crisp-tender, about 2 minutes. Scrape stir-fried vegetables into a medium heatproof bowl; set aside.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Add 1 quart (1L) of the reserved broth to the wok and bring to a boil over high heat. Add rice noodles, stirring to ensure that noodles are wet. Cook, stirring frequently, until noodles soften and turn translucent, about 3 minutes; if noodles appear dry, add more stock in 1/4 cup (60ml) increments.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Return stir-fried vegetables to wok along with shredded chicken and mix well. Lower heat to maintain a simmer. Add oyster sauce, soy sauce, and fish sauce, and stir until sauces are fully incorporated and noodles are coated in a saucy glaze.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Garnish with scallion. Serve immediately with calamansi halves (or serve with a small dish of citrus juice alongside; see note).
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Rice vermicelli noodles can be found in Asian markets, specialty grocery stores, and online. I recommend the brand, Excellent, which contains rice flour, cornstarch, and water.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Calamansi is difficult to source fresh in the US, but occasionally Filipino groceries located in California or Florida will have them. If you can&#39;t find fresh calamansi, you can serve the pancit with a small dish of either calamansi juice (we prefer frozen, as it has a better flavor than bottled options), or a mixture of one part freshly squeezed lemon juice to one-half part freshly squeezed lime juice to one-quarter part granulated sugar (by volume).

Tips:

- The chicken can be prepared in advance and refrigerated for up to 5 days.
- Pancit bihon can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
