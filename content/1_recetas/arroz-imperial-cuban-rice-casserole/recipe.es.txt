Instructions:
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Chicken: In a large pot, place chicken, onion, garlic, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, sazón with coriander and annatto, garlic powder, cumin, oregano, pepper, bay leaf, and 2 quarts cold water. (Chicken should be submerged 2 inches; add more water if necessary.) Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until chicken is cooked through and an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part of breast registers 160°F, about 10 minutes.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Remove from heat. Strain broth and set aside 4 cups broth (store the rest for another use). Using tongs, transfer chicken, onion, and garlic to a large food processor, and add remaining 1/2 teaspoon salt. Let sit for 10 minutes to let chicken cool slightly. Working in batches if needed, pulse until chicken is shredded very fine and has a texture closer to a loose paste, about 1 minute. Wipe pot clean with kitchen towels or paper towels.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: For the Rice: . Add 2 tablespoons olive oil to now-empty pot and heat over medium-low heat until shimmering. Add salt, sazón, garlic powder, and turmeric, and cook until aromatic, about 30 seconds.
  tip: ""
  step_image: 
-
  step_title: ""
  instruction: Stir in rice and cook until rice is coated with seasoning, about 30 seconds. Add reserved 4 cups broth and tomato sauce, and bring to a boil over high heat, scraping bottom of pan to loosen any brown bits. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until rice is fully cooked and sticky but liquid it absorbed, about 16 minutes. Remove from heat. Let rice sit for 10 minutes covered, then fluff rice with fork. Stir in shredded chicken and set aside.
  tip: ""
  step_image:

  instruction: Large pot, food processor, 9x13 baking dish
  tip: ""

  step_title: ""
  instruction: Sazón is a popular seasoning blend in Latin American cooking, typically made with coriander, garlic, cumin, and annatto (achiote), which imparts a warm, golden color and distinctive flavor to dishes. Goya is the most widely available brand in the US, sold in small packets at most supermarkets and Latin grocers, but other brands and homemade blends work just as well.

Tips:

- The unbaked, assembled casserole can be covered and refrigerated for up to 4 days. Follow baking directions above, adding 10 to 15 minutes to the cooking time.
